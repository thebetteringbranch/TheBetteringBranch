Our Impact

Making a difference, one branch at a time since 2024

How our work aligns with the UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

SDG 3: Good Health & Well-Being

We advance community wellness through youth-led, community-centred health initiatives like The Bettering Run
The Bettering Run engaged:
- 200-300 attendees that engaged in learning about health
- Multiple Community partners & booths
- 100% completion rate among registered runners
- The event combined physical activity with educational outreach, featuring curated booths on healthy living and peer-driven health advocacy.
We aim to scale participation by 25% in our next edition!

SDG 4: Quality Education

Through Mission Lighthouse, our weekly academic tutoring programme for primary students (ages 7–12), we work toward equitable learning access.
Programme Metrics:
- Weekly tutoring sessions
- Consistent mentor-student engagement
- Small-group personalised guidance Our focus: long-term educational support, not one-off intervention.

SDG 12: Responsible Consumption & Production

We promote circular economy values through advocacy and direct action.
Through participation in outreach events such as The Wasteless Utopia, we:
- Educated attendees on sustainable fashion policy
- Advocated for reduced textile waste
- Promoted responsible consumption habits

Recycle Red! Initiative:
- Diverted 435 kilograms worth of used red paper packets from landfill
- Mobilised nation-wide recycling participation (30 Schools in 2026 ≈ 30K+ Students)
- Increased youth awareness of festive waste impact

SDG 13: Climate Action

Beyond waste reduction, we engage in policy literacy and environmental advocacy.
At the Sustainable Singapore Gallery Flagship Event, we:
- Educated members of the public on Singapore’s renewable energy policies
- Compared sustainability approaches with countries such as the UK and US
- Encouraged youth-led climate dialogue

SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals

We believe collective challenges require collective solutions.
Through partnerships with corporate organisations and youth-led initiatives, we leverage cross-sector collaboration to scale impact, strengthen community networks, and drive integrated social and environmental change.

RECYCLE RED!

In 2025, We reached out to Geneco and started Recycle Red!, a Chinese New Year sustainability initiative which seeks to promote sustainable & eco-conscious habits during Chinese New Year by partnering with Geneco SG to bring Red Packet Recycling bins to Schools. Recycle Red! is also an inter-school competition, to motivate & reward youths that choose sustainability.

In 2025, we partnered with 13 Junior Colleges to recycle a total of 435 Kilograms worth of red packets. That's the weight of nearly half an elephant! Furthermore, Anderson Serangoon Junior College (ASRJC), recycled 80 kilograms worth of red packets and won a visit from the Milo Van. We thank the Milo Van, Geneco and all our partners for making Recycle Red! 2025 a success!

In 2026, Recycle Red! will be back for its second run. We are excited to see more youths embracing sustainability!

INFOGRAPHIC POSTS ON INSTAGRAM

As part of our mission to "Educate youths on the importance of today's issues", we post educational infographic posts on topics such as Food Insecurity, Pollution, Animal Rights & more. Through our posts, during the height of our activity in April 2025, we gained more than 230K views on our page!

THE BETTERING RUN

We partnered with Canberra Community Club to organise The Bettering Run, a 3.5KM Run + Post-Run Carnival supporting Health & Sustainability. Set against the picturesque backdrop of Canberra Park, we saw more than 200 participants of all ages show up in support of Health & Sustainability.

HOW WE CHAMPIONED HEALTH & SUSTAINABILITY:
- Event decor was largely either made out of recycled materials collected from the community OR personally sourced second-hand from carousell
- Our team came up with creative and engaging game booths that spread awareness about Health &/or Sustainability
-The run itself promoted mental & physical wellbeing as well as walking/running as an alternative to public transport!
Check out our event booklet linked below for more information)

THE WASTELESS UTOPIA

We were invited to The Wasteless Utopia, an event organised by Fashion Parade, held @ SCAPE* in conjunction with the Global Fashion Revolution Week, to share about Fashion Sustainability Policies in Singapore! To tie in with this year's theme of “Think Globally, Act Locally”, we also shared comparisons between Singapore’s Fashion Sustainability Policies VS Other countries like the UK & US, as well as what we can do locally to embrace Sustainable Fashion.

MISSION LIGHTHOUSE

SUSTAINABLE SINGAPORE GALLERY FLAGSHIP EVENT

We partnered with Mastereign and the Sustainable Singapore Gallery to faciliatate tours inside the gallery, conduct eco-friendly activities outside & provide on-site assistance with the event.

Furthermore, we had a booth during the event run by our team to share more about how Singapore can contribute to the global sustainable energy scene with its unique energy policies due to its export-dependent economy & small land size. We also posited the possibilties of Singapore adopting post-growth policies

COMMUNITY CLEAN UPS @ LOCAL PARKS/BEACHES

We organise Community clean ups to do our part to keep SG green & clean. Our clean ups also serve as a way for youths to destress in nature and meet new friends from different schools, supporting youth mental health!
